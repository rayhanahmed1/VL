Temperature

Illustrated as a calendar heat map, the first visual gives an average daily temperature for London from the period 1960 to 2016. Visually for all years, quarter 2 and 3 (specifically May to June) can be seen as the warmest months. January and December are distinctly lighter, therefore colder, compared to the rest of the year. During this time:
  • the coldest temperature recorded was on the 12th Jan 1987 at -6.92°C
  • the hottest temperature recorded was on the 10th August 2003 at 28.82°C
    • Next to the calendar is another heat map, this time a monthly average is used to compare the temperature between months and years. June and July clearly stand out from the rest of the year as the hottest months, and January being the coldest.
    The second visual is a line graph which shows an annual average temperature; calculated using the same dataset. The trend line shows a positive correlation, where there is a gradual temperature rise over the 56-year period. 1961-1962 saw the sharpest drop of 1.68°C (-15%) The late 1980s saw the greatest temperature rise of 2°C (20% increase from 1986 to 1989). 1963 having the lowest average temperature of: 9.67°C and 2014 having the highest, at: 12.86°C, a difference of: 3.19°C
